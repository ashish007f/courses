Data Storage Layer

Architecting the foundation of the modern data platform.

The 4 Stages of Data Storage Evolution

To understand where we are, we have to look at the "Ancestors" of the modern stack.

Traditional Data Warehouse (The Monolith)

Storage and Compute were physically married. To get more storage, you had to buy more CPU. Expensive, rigid, and siloed.

Data Lake (The Raw Frontier)

"Store everything now, figure it out later." It solved the cost problem but created a Data Swamp—no transactions, no performance, and no schema control.

Modern Cloud Data Warehouse (The Decoupled SaaS)

The first to truly separate Compute from Storage. High performance and easy to use, but often uses proprietary formats that "lock" your data.

The Data Lakehouse (The Unified Standard)

The final evolution. It brings the management of a warehouse (ACID, Schema) directly to the open files of a data lake.

The Technical Blueprint: The "Storage Sandwich"

If you look under the hood of a Lakehouse, you see three distinct layers. This is how we achieve "Warehouse speed" on "Lake prices."

The Top (Table Format / The Brain)

Handles "Database" features: ACID transactions, Time Travel, and Schema Evolution. It tells the compute engine exactly which files to read.

The Filling (File Format / The Body)

Optimized columnar storage that ensures we only read the specific columns needed, drastically reducing network I/O.

The Bottom (Object Store / The Foundation)

The physical home for the bytes. It provides virtually infinite scale and high durability at a low cost.

The Organizational Pattern: Medallion Architecture

While the "Sandwich" explains the tech, the Medallion Architecture explains the data quality journey.

Layer State Purpose
🥉 Bronze Raw / Immutable The "Audit" layer. Data is stored exactly as it came from the source. Never delete from here.
🥈 Silver Cleaned / Conformed The "Analyst" layer. PII is masked, data types are cast, and disparate sources are joined.
🥇 Gold Aggregated / Business-Ready The "Executive" layer. Highly optimized tables ready for high-speed reporting and ML.

The Decentralized Pattern: Data Mesh

Moves away from a central "Data Bottleneck" to a distributed model of ownership using a banking context.

Domain Ownership

The Retail Banking team is responsible for the storage and quality of transaction data, as they understand the business context best.

Data as a Product

The Loans team publishes "Credit Risk Scores" as a versioned, documented product with guaranteed uptime (SLOs).

Self-Serve Infrastructure

A central platform team provides the technology stack (the "Storage Sandwich") as a service to the domains.

🏗️ Closing Thought

"We use a Medallion Architecture to manage data quality, sitting on a 3-Layer Sandwich to ensure our storage is as fast as a Traditional Warehouse but as open as a Data Lake."

The Storage Manifesto

"The mission of the storage layer is to turn a 'Data Swamp' into a 'Data Lakehouse.' By decoupling compute from storage and adopting open table formats, we create an immutable foundation that is both performant and future-proof."
